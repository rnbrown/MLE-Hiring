## 1. Executive Summary

This report provides an underwriting analysis of the current merchant portfolio, comprising 50 merchants with a total monthly processing volume of £4,382,000 across 126,130 transactions. The overall portfolio demonstrates a healthy transaction-based dispute rate of 0.0698%.

Key findings indicate a significant concentration of merchants and volume in the United Kingdom, which maintains a dispute rate below the portfolio average. However, certain geographic regions and the internally categorized "High Risk" merchant segment exhibit elevated dispute rates and concentrations of disputes, warranting closer scrutiny. Specifically, countries like Romania, Spain, Ireland, and the United States show higher dispute rates. The "High Risk" merchant segment, while representing only 32% of merchants and 22% of volume, accounts for a disproportionate 51% of all disputes.

Recommendations include implementing enhanced monitoring for high-risk segments and geographies, reviewing underwriting parameters for new applications from these areas, and exploring targeted dispute mitigation strategies to safeguard portfolio quality and minimize potential financial exposure.

## 4. Risk Distribution Analysis

The portfolio is segmented into internal risk categories (High, Medium, Low), revealing a clear disparity in dispute contribution across these segments.

| Risk Level | Merchants | Volume (£)   | Disputes | Disputes per £1M Volume |
| :--------- | :-------- | :----------- | :------- | :---------------------- |
| High       | 16        | 978,000      | 45       | **46.01**               |
| Medium     | 18        | 2,119,000    | 39       | 18.40                   |
| Low        | 16        | 1,285,000    | 4        | 3.11                    |

**Observations:**

*   **High Risk Segment:** This segment accounts for 32% of merchants and 22% of the total portfolio volume but is responsible for a disproportionate 51% of all disputes (45 out of 88). The "Disputes per £1M Volume" metric clearly highlights this, with High Risk merchants generating 46.01 disputes per £1M volume, significantly higher than Medium (18.40) and Low (3.11) risk segments.
*   **Medium Risk Segment:** While representing the largest share of volume (48%) and a significant portion of merchants (36%), its dispute contribution (44%) is relatively proportional to its volume share.
*   **Low Risk Segment:** This segment performs exceptionally well, contributing 29% of the volume but only 5% of the total disputes, indicating robust merchant health and low-risk operations.

The concentration of disputes within the "High Risk" category suggests that internal risk classification is effective in identifying problematic merchants, but also points to a need for specific risk mitigation strategies for this segment.

## 5. Key Observations

Based on the analysis of the portfolio statistics, the following key observationsv are put forward:

### Key Observations:

1.  **Overall Portfolio Health:** The overall portfolio dispute rate of 0.0698% is relatively low, indicating a generally healthy portfolio.
2.  **Geographic Concentration & Stability:** The United Kingdom forms the stable core of the portfolio, contributing the majority of merchants and volume with a dispute rate below the portfolio average.
3.  **Emerging Geographic Risks:** Specific countries such as Romania, Spain, Ireland, and the United States show elevated dispute rates. While these may represent smaller volume shares individually, their higher risk profile could impact overall portfolio health if not managed proactively.
4.  **High-Risk Segment Disparity:** The "High Risk" merchant segment is a primary driver of disputes, accounting for over half of all disputes despite representing only 22% of the total monthly volume. This strongly validates the internal risk segmentation model.
5.  **Small Merchant Volume & Disputes:** Many countries with only one merchant show a diverse range of dispute performance, from zero disputes to very high rates, suggesting individual merchant characteristics are crucial.
